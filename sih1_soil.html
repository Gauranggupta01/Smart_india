Soil Health for Sustainable Farming

Learn how to maintain and improve soil health for long-term agricultural productivity and sustainability.

Why Soil Health is Important?

Soil health is the foundation of productive farming. Healthy soil promotes better crop yields, improves water retention, reduces the need for chemical fertilizers, and supports the environment.

Soil Testing and Analysis

Regular soil testing helps farmers understand the nutrient composition of their soil, pH levels, and any deficiencies. This enables better decision-making when applying fertilizers and managing soil quality.

Find Soil Testing Centers

Best Practices for Improving Soil Health

  • Use organic fertilizers to enhance nutrient levels.
  • Practice crop rotation to prevent nutrient depletion.
  • Implement no-till or minimum-till farming to maintain soil structure.
  • Use cover crops to prevent soil erosion and improve organic matter.

The government offers various schemes to support soil health management, including subsidies for organic fertilizers, soil testing programs, and farmer education initiatives.
